Australia’s Flight Network

Australia's flight network is the backbone of our connectivity as a nation. Being the sixth largest country in the world, lots of cities are separated by large distances, making air travel the primary mode of transport of travelling between one city to the other. The routes between Melbourne, Sydney and Brisbane represent some of the busiest routes, not just in Australia but in the world. The following visualisation will further explore areas of Australia's immense flight network, showcasing trends and distruptions that have occurred.

The Map

The map highlights how air traffic reflects where people live and do business. The strong east-coast routes show how concentrated Australia’s population and economy are, while thinner lines to Perth and Darwin remind us how far and isolated those cities are.

You can also see how the network responds to major events. The sharp fall in 2020 captures the impact of COVID-19 lockdowns, and the recovery in later years shows how quickly air travel bounced back. Overall, the trends here show how air travel not only connects cities but also reflects the country’s resilience and changing travel habits.
